SALES PLAYBOOK
===============

This playbook defines our sales methodology, stage-specific guidance, qualification frameworks, and best practices aligned to our product portfolio and target industries.


SALES STAGES
=============

Our pipeline follows seven stages, matching our CRM deal tracking system:

1. PROSPECTING (10% probability)
2. QUALIFICATION (20% probability)
3. NEEDS ANALYSIS (30% probability)
4. VALUE PROPOSITION (40% probability)
5. PROPOSAL (60% probability)
6. NEGOTIATION (80% probability)
7. CLOSED WON / CLOSED LOST (100% / 0%)


STAGE 1: PROSPECTING
=====================
Objective: Identify potential customers and initiate first contact.

Activities:
- Research target account using CRM data (industry, company size, annual revenue)
- Identify key decision makers (CTO, CIO, VP Engineering, VP Operations)
- Craft personalized outreach referencing industry-specific pain points
- Leverage existing customer references in the same industry

Prospecting by Industry:
- Technology: Lead with Cloud Platform and Compute Instance. Reference scaling challenges.
- Finance: Lead with compliance capabilities (SOX, PCI-DSS). Reference Storage Array latency.
- Healthcare: Lead with HIPAA compliance and BAA availability. Reference Smart Innovations case study.
- Government: Lead with FedRAMP authorization. Reference Future Innovations case study.
- Energy: Lead with data analytics and predictive maintenance. Reference Future Corp case study.
- Manufacturing: Lead with Workstation ISV certifications and ERP integration.
- Retail: Lead with CDN Service and auto-scaling for traffic spikes.
- Education: Lead with pricing flexibility and TCO savings. Reference deduplication savings.
- Transportation: Lead with Enterprise Suite fleet management capabilities.

Outreach Templates:

For Technology Prospects:
Subject: Reducing cloud infrastructure complexity at [Company Name]
Body: Our Cloud Platform consolidates container orchestration, serverless compute, and managed databases into a single platform. Companies like Global Corp have consolidated 7 separate tools into one. Would a 20-minute overview be valuable?

For Healthcare Prospects:
Subject: HIPAA-compliant project management for [Company Name]
Body: Enterprise Suite includes built-in HIPAA audit trails, encryption, and BAA support. Smart Innovations deployed it for their telemedicine platform in just 6 weeks. Can I share how it could support your compliance requirements?

For Government Prospects:
Subject: FedRAMP-authorized cloud platform for [Agency Name]
Body: Our Cloud Platform holds FedRAMP authorization and our Enterprise Suite is SOC 2 Type II certified. We helped Future Innovations modernize their citizen-facing services with zero unplanned downtime. Would it be helpful to discuss your modernization priorities?


STAGE 2: QUALIFICATION
=======================
Objective: Determine if the opportunity is worth pursuing.

Use the BANT Framework:
- Budget: Does the prospect have allocated budget? What is the expected deal size?
  - Deals under $100K: Typically Professional Edition, Online Course, or single-product sales
  - Deals $100K-$500K: Multi-product deals or consulting engagements
  - Deals over $500K: Enterprise-wide deployments or multi-year contracts
- Authority: Are we talking to the decision maker or an influencer?
  - Technology decisions: CTO or VP Engineering
  - Infrastructure decisions: CIO or VP Infrastructure
  - Operational efficiency: COO or VP Operations
  - Compliance-driven: CISO or Compliance Officer
- Need: Is there a clear pain point our products address?
  - Map pain points to product capabilities using the Industry Solutions Guide
- Timeline: Is there a compelling event driving urgency?
  - Contract renewal dates, compliance deadlines, incident recovery, fiscal year budgets

Qualification Criteria (must meet at least 3 of 4):
- Budget confirmed or budget cycle identified
- Decision maker identified and engaged
- Clear pain point mapped to our product portfolio
- Timeline within 6 months

Red Flags:
- "Just looking" with no timeline or compelling event
- Unable to identify or access the decision maker after 3 attempts
- Pain points outside our product portfolio
- Active competing RFP where we weren't involved in requirements definition


STAGE 3: NEEDS ANALYSIS
========================
Objective: Deeply understand the prospect's requirements and environment.

Discovery Questions by Product Category:

Software Licenses (Enterprise Suite, Professional Edition, Cloud Platform):
- How many users will need access? What roles and access levels?
- What tools are you currently using? What's working, what isn't?
- What integrations are required (SSO, ERP, development tools)?
- What compliance requirements apply (SOX, HIPAA, FedRAMP)?
- What's your current workflow for [specific process]?

Cloud Services (Compute Instance, Cloud Storage, CDN Service):
- What workloads will run on the infrastructure?
- What are your performance requirements (latency, throughput, IOPS)?
- What's your current cloud spend and growth trajectory?
- Do you have multi-region or disaster recovery requirements?
- What's your data volume and growth rate?

Hardware (Network Switch, Storage Array, Server Rack, Workstation):
- What's your current network topology and bandwidth utilization?
- What storage protocols are in use (NFS, iSCSI, FC, NVMe)?
- What's your data center power and cooling capacity?
- What existing infrastructure must the new hardware integrate with?
- What's the expected data growth over the next 3 years?

Consulting (Strategy Consulting, Migration Service, Optimization Package):
- What's the scope of the transformation initiative?
- What systems and applications are in scope for migration?
- What's your internal team's capacity for the project?
- What's the risk tolerance for downtime during migration?
- Have you attempted this before? What happened?

Training (Technical Training, Online Course, Certification Program, On-site Workshop):
- How many staff need training? What are their current skill levels?
- Is training for a new deployment or ongoing skill development?
- Do you prefer instructor-led or self-paced delivery?
- Are certifications required by contract or regulation?

Support (Maintenance Contract, Premium Support, 24/7 Monitoring, SLA Guarantee):
- What are your current SLA requirements (uptime, response time)?
- Do you have 24/7 internal IT staff or is after-hours coverage a gap?
- What's the business impact of a 1-hour outage? A 4-hour outage?
- What monitoring tools are currently in use?


STAGE 4: VALUE PROPOSITION
===========================
Objective: Present a tailored solution that directly addresses identified needs.

Value Proposition Framework:
1. Restate the prospect's pain points (prove you listened)
2. Map each pain point to specific product capabilities
3. Quantify the impact (cost savings, time savings, risk reduction)
4. Reference a relevant case study from the same industry
5. Differentiate against the likely competitive alternative

Sample Value Propositions:

For a Technology Company evaluating Cloud Platform:
"You mentioned your team spends 30% of their time managing infrastructure instead of building features. Cloud Platform's managed Kubernetes and serverless capabilities would eliminate that overhead. Based on your team size, that's approximately $400K in recovered engineering productivity annually. Global Corp saw similar results after consolidating from 7 tools to our single platform."

For a Healthcare Organization evaluating Enterprise Suite:
"Your current project tracking doesn't meet HIPAA audit trail requirements, which puts you at risk during your next compliance audit. Enterprise Suite includes built-in HIPAA audit trails, encryption at rest, and we provide a BAA. Smart Innovations deployed it for their telemedicine platform and achieved HIPAA compliance without any additional third-party tools, saving them an estimated $150K in compliance tooling costs."

For a Government Agency evaluating Cloud Platform + Migration Service:
"Your citizen portal experienced 15 outages last year, with the longest lasting 8 hours. Our Cloud Platform with FedRAMP authorization and multi-region deployment would virtually eliminate unplanned downtime. Future Innovations achieved zero unplanned downtime in their first 12 months after migration, and we can deliver the migration with sub-hour RTO using our Migration Service team."


STAGE 5: PROPOSAL
==================
Objective: Deliver a formal proposal with pricing and implementation plan.

Proposal Structure:
1. Executive Summary — why this solution, why now, why us
2. Solution Architecture — products, configuration, integration points
3. Implementation Plan — phases, timeline, milestones, resource requirements
4. Pricing — line items by product, discounts applied, payment terms
5. ROI Analysis — projected cost savings, productivity gains, risk reduction
6. Customer References — relevant case studies from same industry
7. Terms and Conditions — licensing, SLA, support, renewal

Pricing Guidelines:
- Software Licenses: Annual subscription, 3-year commits get 15% discount
- Cloud Services: Monthly billing with annual commit discount (20-30%)
- Hardware: One-time purchase with optional financing (36-month terms)
- Consulting: Fixed-fee per engagement, not T&M unless scope is uncertain
- Training: Per-session pricing with volume discounts for 5+ sessions
- Support: Annual contracts, auto-renew, price locked for contract term

Common Bundling Strategies:
- "Full Stack": Enterprise Suite + Cloud Platform + Premium Support (15% bundle discount)
- "Infrastructure Modernization": Hardware + Migration Service + Technical Training (10% bundle discount)
- "Cloud Adoption": Cloud Services + Strategy Consulting + Certification Program (12% bundle discount)
- "Operational Excellence": 24/7 Monitoring + SLA Guarantee + Maintenance Contract (10% bundle discount)


STAGE 6: NEGOTIATION
=====================
Objective: Reach mutually acceptable terms and close the deal.

Negotiation Principles:
- Never discount without getting something in return (longer commitment, larger scope, reference-ability)
- Discount authority: Sales Reps can offer up to 10%. Manager approval for 10-20%. VP approval for 20%+.
- Alternative to discounting: offer additional training, extended support, or faster implementation

Common Objections and Responses:

"Your price is too high."
Response: Focus on TCO, not sticker price. Example: "Storage Array's deduplication typically achieves 4:1 data reduction. You'd need to buy 4x the raw capacity from [competitor] to store the same data, making their lower unit price actually more expensive overall. In the Next Technologies deal, our TCO was 45% lower than the competing NetApp proposal over 3 years."

"We're happy with our current solution."
Response: Ask about specific pain points identified in discovery. "You mentioned quarterly reporting takes 3 days. After migrating to Cloud Platform, Future Systems reduced that to 4 hours. Is that improvement worth exploring?"

"We need to see a pilot first."
Response: Agree, but structure it. "Absolutely — I'd suggest a 60-day pilot at a single location, similar to what we did with Innovate Corp. They started with one distribution center and the results drove enterprise-wide expansion. Let's define success criteria upfront so we can move quickly if the pilot meets expectations."

"Your competitor offered a lower price."
Response: "Can I understand what's included in that price? We've found that competing proposals often exclude implementation, training, and premium support that are bundled in our offer. When Innovate Solutions compared apples-to-apples, our proposal was $180K less over 3 years despite appearing higher on the line-item price."

"We don't have budget until next fiscal year."
Response: "I understand budget timing. Let's do two things: first, let me get you a detailed ROI analysis you can use in your budget request — Future Corp's 15% energy output improvement translated to $3M in annual revenue, and their approval took just one executive meeting. Second, we can structure payment to start in Q1 of your next fiscal year while beginning implementation now."


DEAL VELOCITY BENCHMARKS
=========================

By Industry:
- Technology: 4-8 weeks average
- Consulting: 4-8 weeks average
- Retail: 6-10 weeks average
- Manufacturing: 6-12 weeks average
- Education: 8-14 weeks average
- Energy: 8-12 weeks average
- Transportation: 6-10 weeks average
- Media: 4-8 weeks average
- Healthcare: 10-20 weeks average
- Real Estate: 8-14 weeks average
- Finance: 8-16 weeks average
- Government: 12-36 weeks average (6-18 months)

By Deal Size:
- Under $100K: 4-6 weeks
- $100K - $300K: 6-10 weeks
- $300K - $500K: 8-14 weeks
- Over $500K: 12-20 weeks

Deals Exceeding Benchmarks:
If a deal is taking significantly longer than these benchmarks, assess:
- Is the decision maker engaged, or are we stuck at an influencer level?
- Has a compelling event (compliance deadline, contract renewal, incident) been identified?
- Is there a competing priority consuming the prospect's attention?
- Should we re-scope to a smaller initial deal with expansion potential?


CROSS-SELL AND EXPANSION PLAYBOOK
===================================

After Closing Software Licenses:
- At 90 days: Propose Technical Training or On-site Workshop for user adoption
- At 6 months: Propose Maintenance Contract or Premium Support for ongoing value
- At 12 months: Propose Cloud Platform or Cloud Services for infrastructure alignment

After Closing Cloud Services:
- At 90 days: Propose 24/7 Monitoring for production workloads
- At 6 months: Propose SLA Guarantee as usage grows and uptime becomes critical
- At 12 months: Propose Optimization Package to right-size and reduce spend

After Closing Hardware:
- At 30 days: Propose Technical Training for the operations team
- At 90 days: Propose Maintenance Contract
- At 6 months: Propose Migration Service if legacy infrastructure remains
- At 12 months: Propose additional hardware for capacity expansion

After Closing Consulting:
- At engagement completion: Propose the products recommended in the consulting deliverable
- At 6 months: Propose a follow-up Optimization Package to tune the implementation
- At 12 months: Propose Training and Certification for the customer's team

- Innovate Corp (Transportation): $200K single-site pilot → $646K enterprise deployment (3.2x expansion)
